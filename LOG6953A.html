LOG6953A: Human-Centered Inquiry for Software and Computer Engineering (Winter 2019)

LOG6953A is an advanced undergraduate/graduate course offered in English at Polytechnique Montreal by Dr. Jinghui Cheng ( jinghui.cheng [at] polymtl [dot] ca). This course aims to equip students with human-centered research methods for understanding and supporting computer and software users, developers, designers, and other stakeholders. These research methods are especially essential in empirical software engineering and human-computer interaction.

At the end of the course, the students will be able to:

  • analyze the research design strategies, logistical considerations, and ethical concerns of human-centered inquiry in the context of software and computer engineering;
  • design and execute common human-centered studies and evaluate their advantages and disadvantages;
  • select and perform appropriate analysis techniques to examine data collected from human-centered studies, and;
  • effectively summarize and communicate human-centered research findings in software and computer engineering.

The course will cover the following topics:

Introduction
- Motivations for doing human-centered research in software and computer engineering
- Overview of human-centered research methods used in software and computer engineering
- Exemplar for methods overview: Supporting the design process of serious games
 
Ethical concerns when working with human subjects
- A brief introduction to the Tri-council policy statement: ethical conduct for research involving humans
- A brief introduction to the ACM Code of Ethics and Professional Conduct
- Related regulations and legislation: Canada's Anti-Spam Act, General Data Protection Regulation
- Requirements from the research ethics committee
 
Structured observation of usage and development activities
- Introduction of ethnography and contextual inquiry
- Exemplar: Observation studies of software designers and developers
- Exemplar: Observation studies about smart home technologies
- Exemplar: Observation studies about technologies in medical and health domains
 
Interview for understanding users/developers and eliciting requirements
- Interview planning and administration
- Generating and presenting user requirements from interview results
- Exemplar: Understanding users who have special needs (e.g. children, elderly, people with disabilities)
- Exemplar: Understanding the needs of software designers and developers
 
Survey for collecting large-scale user or developer-centered data
- Survey construction and administration
- Exemplar: Surveying users who have special needs (e.g. children, elderly, people with disabilities)
- Exemplar: Surveying usability and user experience designers
- Exemplar: Surveying open source developers
 
Lab-based human-centered study for evaluating computer and software systems
- Lab-based study design and administration
- A typical layout of a human-centered lab
- Exemplar: Lab-based evaluation of software engineering tools 
- Exemplar: Lab-based study of user interaction with robotics and mixed reality technologies
- Exemplar: Lab-based games user research
 
Analysis of human-centered data
- Overview of types of data
- Inductive and deductive qualitative data analysis techniques: qualitative coding, affinity diagraming (card sorting), grounded theory
- Statistical approaches in analyzing quantitative data: comparing the means, inter-rater reliability
- Reporting and presentation of human-centered results in software and computer engineering
 
Other human-centered inquiry methods
- Analyzing open data about software developers and other stakeholders
- Experience sampling and diary studies
- Using physiological tools to understand user experience
- Creating user profiles using telemetry and log data
