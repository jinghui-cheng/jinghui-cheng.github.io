Information regarding the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) in the context of the research project titled "How Open Source Communities Work Towards Usability Issues"

About the research project

The objectives of this study are as follows: (i) exploring how the open source application software communities currently raise, discuss, negotiate, and eventually address these issues, (ii) investigating their perceived challenges in these activities and (iii) understanding how characteristics of open source practitioners (including their technical and cultural background, as well as motivational tendencies) have affected their perceptions and practices towards the usability issues. In order to reach our objectives, we intend to conduct a survey with open source software practitioners and community participants. This study serves as a first step in a larger project that aims to develop models, techniques, and systems to support software practitioners to consider and address usability requirements in an interdisciplinary and distributed environment.

Information regarding the processing of personal data

  • The personal data is collected by École Polytechnique de Montréal, through Prof. Jinghui Cheng (jinghui.cheng@polymtl.ca), and McGill University, through Prof. Jin L.C. Guo (jguo@cs.mcgill.ca)
  • The personal data is processed in the course and for the purpose of a research project. This processing is justified by the terms of the consent given by the data subject to GitHub, as per the GitHub Terms of Service and the GitHub Privacy Statement (version of May 25th, 2018).
  • The recipients of the personal data are limited to the research team and an online data storage application.
  • The personal data is to be stored in Canada and in the United States of America.
  • The personal data is to be stored for a period of 10 years.
  • You have the following rights with regards to your personal data: right of access, right to rectification or erasure, right to restriction of processing and right to data portability.
  • You have the right to withdraw your consent to the processing of your personal data, which does not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.
  • You have the right to lodge a complaint with a relevant supervisory authority.
  • You are not obliged to provide any personal data while completing the survey. However, the compensation is only going to be available if you complete the survey.
  • The decision whether to contact or not to contact potential participants to the research was based on an automated decision-making process that selects the main contributors to the code and issue discussions of open source projects that includes a sophisticated user interface; these open source projects were selected manually by the researchers.
